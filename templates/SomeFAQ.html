Welcome to the FAQ page for the Climate Change Tracker Website. Here you will find some of the most commonly asked questions about climate change along with answers/plausible solutions to those questions.

Beforehand, how did we know what greenhouse gas and temperature levels were like in the far past?

Scientists can utilize ice cores to get historical temperature data. Using the study of tree wood rings, scientists can reveal temperature data from up to two thousand years ago. Oceanic coral have age rings that can be analyzed as well.

What is the difference between global warming and climate change?

The biggest difference between the two terms is that global warming is related to the long term warming of Earth. Climate Change is a much more broad term that refers to global warming and other types of geological changes to the world like increasing sea levels, shrinking glaciers, and ice melts.

What exactly is the greenhouse effect?

This greenhouse effect is where the Earth's heat is trapped closeby the surface. The heat is trapped by the presence of greenhouse gases from the ozone layer that cause internally increasing temperatures on Earth.
